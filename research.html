Postdoctoral Research

Taming quantum fluctuation-induced phenomena

My postdoctoral research investigates quantum fluctuation-induced phenomena: quantum effects that become increasingly important at microscopic and nanoscopic scales. I study how these effects influence interactions between particles and macroscopic surfaces, with the aim of improving the design and reliability of micro- and nanoscale devices.

PhD Research

Polariton Dynamics in Ultra-Confined Optical Cavities: A Study of Interaction Effects and Coherence Properties

My PhD research investigates the interaction between light and matter in ultra-confined optical cavities, focusing on interaction effects and coherence properties in polaritonic systems. As part of this work, I developed an algorithm for incorporating particle correlations to arbitrary order of precision in the analysis.
